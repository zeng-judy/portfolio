CTA 201 Bus Drops in Ridership after Northwestern Ends Ventra Program

The CTA 201 bus on a snowy day. Source: Evanston Now
Trouble viewing the image? Download it here.
By Judy Zeng
March 1, 2025

After Northwestern ended the Chicago Transit Authority 201 Ventra card program last June, the 201 bus ridership has dropped significantly in the fall academic quarter. From September 2024 to November 2024, ridership was 9,000 fewer on average compared to 2023.

Have trouble seeing the chart? Download it here.

CTA 201 ridership appears to fluctuate with the academic year, peaking during the academic quarters and dipping during the summer months.

The university cited that the Intercampus shuttle was sufficient to meet the needs of community members in an email last August. However, the Northwestern community appears to have made use of the program, as the cancellation of the program appears to be correlated with a drop in ridership levels.

According to the official CTA Facebook video report, a major goal in 2024 was to increase service and ridership to pre-pandemic levels. Last year, CTA buses provided nearly 182 million rides, which is part of a year-over-year ridership increase of 12%.
